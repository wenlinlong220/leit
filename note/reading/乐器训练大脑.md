## 错题梳理

- Q1：The studies mentioned in the first paragraph ?
- D. cast doubt on a belief about the effects of taking music lessons.
- B. present misleading information about the value of learning an instrument.

- 原句：The studies mentioned in the first paragraph

:::analysis
复盘：prensent这里代表的是展示，当时理解成了揭露了一种误导性的信息，实际上选项意思是文章写了一个误导性的内容，我想的那种意思应该对应的是cast doubt

考点：cast doubt 和 present
:::end-analysis

:::answer
答案：D
:::end-answer

---

- Q2：What do Hanna-Pladdy's studies suggest about people's general education?
- A. Studying music compensates for an otherwise limited level of education.
- B. Learning to play an instrument improves educational performance.
- 原句：Hanna-Pladdy ==suspects== that musical training could have made up for the lack of cognitive stimulation these people had.

:::analysis
复盘：主要是made up的意思不熟悉，这里应该是组成的意思。compensate是弥补的意思，A选项的otherwise很奇怪，应该理解成，学习音乐弥补了他们若非没有音乐就非常有限的一个教育水平
考点：compensate <=> made up, lack <=> limited
:::end-analysis

:::answer
答案：A
:::end-answer

- Q3：Nina Kraus believes there is a link between better hearing in later life and the experience of
- F. having played an instrument for a considerable length of time.
- E. discovering which group of people becomes the best musicians.

- 原句：Although none of the subjects had played a musical instrument in 40 years, those who had trained the longest—between four and fourteen years—responded the fastest. 'That's significant,' says Kraus, 'because hearing tends to decline as we age

:::analysis
复盘：当时看到E有比较或者说最高级，原文中有一个fastest就选了。实际上这里应该考虑到题目信息，题目说到听力问题，所以定位到这里，然后提到了训练最长时间的听力更好
考点：considerable 相当大的
:::end-s

:::answer
答案 F
:::end-answer
