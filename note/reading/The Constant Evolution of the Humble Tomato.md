# The Constant Evolution of the Humble Tomato 全题解析

:::exam-left
**The Constant Evolution of the Humble Tomato**

**A** The heirloom tomato has become a symbol of everything that is right about growing food the old-fashioned way. Heirloom tomatoes come in a rainbow of colors and an array of shapes and sizes. They taste wonderful. But the heirloom tomato enthusiast might conclude that these varieties represent the full range of genetic diversity in the tomato. The irony is that they don't. The heirloom tomato is actually a very narrow slice of the genetic diversity available in the tomato family.

**B** The tomato originated in the Andes of South America, where it grew wild. It was first cultivated by the Aztecs and other peoples in Mexico. The Spanish brought it to Europe in the 1500s, and from there it spread to the rest of the world. The tomato was initially regarded with suspicion in Europe, partly because it belongs to the nightshade family, which includes some poisonous plants.

**C** Scientists have been trying to find the original wild ancestor of the modern tomato. The closest relative is the currant tomato, which, based on genetic comparisons, split from today's tomato some 1.4 million years ago. The currant tomato is tiny—about the size of a pea—and grows wild in South America.

**D** Researchers have found that the tomato underwent a dramatic transformation during domestication. Tanksley and his colleagues reported that they found a gene that increases fruit size by 50 percent. It was probably the most important event in domestication. The first written record of tomatoes—from Spain in the 1500s—confirms that this mutation, which enlarges tomatoes by producing compartments known as locules, existed back in the same yellow tomatoes that gave Italians the word pomodoro, or golden apple.

**E** The heirloom tomato has a wonderful flavor, but it has a serious weakness: disease resistance. They often suffer from infections that cause the fruit to crack, split, and otherwise rot quickly. Wild plants must continuously evolve to fend off such infections, points out Roger Chetelat of the Tomato Genetics Resource Center at the University of California. But in their quest for size, shape, and flavor, humans have inadvertently eliminated defensive genes. As a result, most possess only a single disease-resistant gene. Chetelat elaborates that heirlooms' taste may have less to do with their genes than with the productivity of the plant and the growing environment. Heirloom tomatoes are typically grown in small quantities and allowed to ripen on the vine, which concentrates their flavor.

**F** In 1996, a tomato breeder and former Tanksley student named Doug Heath began a favorite project. Over 12 years, he crossed heirloom tomatoes with wild relatives to breed disease resistance back into them. He created a new multi-colored tomato less prone to cracking and also endowed with 12 disease-resistant genes. The heirloom's defects are, after all, just an accident of a narrow breeding strategy left over from the very beginning of genetic modification.

**Q24: In regard to genetic similarities, the type of tomato __________ is the nearest to the earliest.**

- 定位词：`genetic`、`nearest`（最独特）
- 原文（段C）：The **closest relative** is the **currant tomato**, which, based on **genetic comparisons**, split from today's tomato some 1.4 million years ago.

| 题目词 | 原文词 | 替换类型 |
|--------|--------|---------|
| similarities | comparisons | 概念相关替换（比较的目的是看相似性） |
| nearest | closest | 同义词替换 |
| the earliest | split...1.4 million years ago | 概念展开（最早分裂 = 最接近原始祖先） |

- 空格位置：`the type of tomato [空格] is`，空格在 tomato 和 is 之间，需要填**修饰 tomato 的词**（名词定语）
- ⚠️ **为什么不填 relative？**
  - `relative` 是名词，放在 `the type of tomato` 后面语法不通
  - `relative`（亲缘关系）的含义已经被题目中的 `nearest to the earliest` 表达出来了，不需要再填
  - 空格需要的是**哪种番茄**，答案是 `currant`（醋栗番茄）
- ⚠️ **currant 和 tomato 顺序问题：**
  - 原文：`the currant tomato`（currant 在前修饰 tomato）
  - 题目：`the type of tomato [currant]`（tomato 提前，currant 后置说明）
  - 这是正常的句子改写，意思完全相同
- ✅ 答案：**currant**

---

**Q25: A genetic __________, which is evident in pomodoro, produced larger tomatoes.**

- 定位词：`pomodoro`（最独特，直接定位）
- 原文（段D）：this **mutation**, which enlarges tomatoes by producing compartments known as locules, existed back in the same yellow tomatoes that gave Italians the word **pomodoro**

- 空格位置：`A genetic [空格]`，前有冠词 `A` + 形容词 `genetic`，空格填**名词**
- 候选词分析：

| 候选词 | 分析 |
|--------|------|
| `gene` | 基因是静态存在，本身不"产生"更大的番茄 |
| `mutation` | 基因突变是**变化事件**，它才能"产生"结果 |

- `genetic mutation`（基因突变）是固定搭配，语法和语义完全合适
- 原文明确说 `this mutation, which enlarges tomatoes`，不是 `this gene enlarges tomatoes`
- ✅ 答案：**mutation**

---

**Q26: __________ are a problem for heirloom tomatoes because they frequently lead to damage and deterioration.**

- 定位词：`heirloom tomatoes`、`damage`
- 原文（段E）：They often **suffer from infections** that **cause** the fruit to crack, split, and otherwise rot quickly.

| 题目词 | 原文词 | 替换类型 |
|--------|--------|---------|
| lead to | cause | 同义词替换 |
| damage and deterioration | crack, split, rot | 概念概括替换 |

- 空格位置：句首主语，后接 `are a problem`，需要填**复数名词**
- ⚠️ **为什么不填 germ-beating（genes）？**
  - **理由一（你问的）**：题目说"are a problem"（是负面的），但 `germ-beating genes` 是抗菌基因，原文说它 `won't harm`（不会造成损害），是**正面的**，语义矛盾
  - **理由二（更根本）**：题目要求 ONE WORD ONLY，`germ-beating genes` 是两个词，直接排除
- `infections`（感染）是负面的，是造成损害的原因，完全符合
- ✅ 答案：**infections**

## 句子精析

### 句一：
**原句**：
They often suffer from ==infections== that cause the fruit to ==crack, split, and otherwise rot quickly==.

**单词**：
1. infection n.感染
2. crack v.破裂  n.裂缝
3. split v.分裂
4. rot v.腐败 n.腐烂物

---
### 句二：

**原句：**
Wild plants must continuously evolve to fend off such infections, points out Roger Chetelat of the Tomato Genetics Resource Center at the University of California. 

**结构拆解:**
- **[引述内容]**: "Wild plants must continuously evolve..." (作为 points out 的逻辑宾语)
- **谓语 (Verb)**: points out (指出)
- **主语 (Subject)**: Roger Chetelat (加上后面一长串的头衔/机构名)

**为什么倒装?**
- **避免头重脚轻 (End-weight Principle):** 当主语带有很长的修饰成分时，将动词提前可以使句子结构更平衡，读者能更快抓住"谁做了什么"。
- **新闻/学术风格:** 这种先说观点、后交代出处的写法在正式文体中非常普遍。

"...," said the spokesperson. （发言人说……）
"……"，发言人说道。（发言人说……）

"...," argues the scientist. （科学家辩称……）
"……"，科学家如此论述。（科学家辩称……）

"...," notes the author. （作者指出……）
"……"，作者指出。（作者写道……）

**词汇**：
- fend off ：抵御 

---
### 句三：

**原句**：
 But in their ==quest== for size, shape, and flavor, humans have ==inadvertently== ==eliminated defensive== genes.

**词汇**：
1. inadvertently
```Advertise (广告/宣传)
ad- (向/往) + vert (转) + -ise (动词后缀)
字面意思： 使人们的注意力"转向"某个东西。
逻辑： 所谓"打广告"，就是为了让你转过头来看这个产品。
```
```
Inadvertently (不经意地/非故意地)
in- (不) + ad- (向) + vert (转) + -ent (形容词后缀) + -ly (副词后缀)
字面意思： 你的心没有"转向"那一处。
逻辑： 当你做某事的时候，你的注意力并没有"转向"那个动作，也就是你没留神、没注意。
结果： 所以这个词翻译为"不经意地"或"无心之举"。
```
2. quest n.寻求
3. eliminate v.剔除，根除

---
### 句四：
**原句**：
As a result, most possess only a single disease-resistant gene. 

**单词**：
1. possess v.拥有  <=> process n.步骤，程序
2. disease-resistant 抗病的
3. as a result 因此

---
### 句五：
**原句**：
Chetelat elaborates that heirlooms' taste may have less to do with their genes than with the productivity of the plant and the growing environment. 

**语言点**：
1. have to do with 整体就等于 "与……有关"

    have a lot to do with... → 关系很大
    与……关系很大

    have something to do with... → 有点关系
    与……有点关系

    have little to do with... → 没什么关系
    与……没什么关系 → 没什么关系

    have nothing to do with... → 毫无关系
    与……毫无关系
2. elaborate v.阐述，精心制作
    e + labor + ate 辛苦劳动出来

---

### 句六（F段尾句）：
**原句**：
The heirloom's defects are, after all, just an accident of a narrow breeding strategy left over from the very beginning of genetic modification.

**结构拆解：**
- 主干：The heirloom's defects **are** just an accident
- `after all`：插入语，表示"毕竟/说到底"
- `of a narrow breeding strategy`：介词短语，定语修饰 accident（什么样的意外）
- `left over from the very beginning of genetic modification`：过去分词短语，后置定语修饰 strategy（遗留自基因改造最初阶段的育种策略）

**含义：**
heirloom 的缺陷，说到底不过是人类最初进行基因改造时，采用了过于狭窄的育种策略所留下的意外产物。

**词汇：**
- `after all` 毕竟，说到底
- `narrow` adj. 狭窄的
- `breeding strategy` 育种策略
- `left over` 遗留下来的
- `genetic modification` 基因改造

## 本题错题规律总结

| 题号 | 易错点 | 核心教训 |
|------|--------|---------|
| Q24 | 想填 relative | `relative` 的含义已被 `nearest to the earliest` 替换，空格需要的是**哪种番茄**（名词定语），填 `currant` |
| Q24 | currant/tomato 顺序 | 原文前置定语 `currant tomato` → 题目改写为 `the type of tomato [currant]`，顺序变了但意思相同 |
| Q24 | similarities ≠ comparisons | 两者不是同义词，是**概念相关替换**：做比较（comparison）的目的是看相似性（similarity） |
| Q25 | gene vs mutation | `gene` 是静态存在，`mutation` 是变化事件；题目说"produced larger tomatoes"，需要的是**事件**，填 `mutation` |
| Q26 | 想填 germ-beating | ① `won't harm` 说明它是正面的，不是"problem" ② 要填名词直接排除 |
| 段落匹配 | 找中间某句话 | 段落匹配匹配的是**段落整体主题**，不是某一句话；先总结每段主题，再对应题目 |
