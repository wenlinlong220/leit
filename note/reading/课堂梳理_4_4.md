# IELTS Reading：T/F/NG 判断题 课堂梳理

---

## 一、判断题两种题型区别

### A 类（事实类判断）
- 题目要求：判断陈述是否与 **文章信息 (information)** 一致
- 答案选项：**TRUE / FALSE / NOT GIVEN**

### B 类（观点类判断）
- 题目要求：判断陈述是否与 **作者观点/声称 (views/claims)** 一致
- 答案选项：**YES / NO / NOT GIVEN**

> ⚠️ 两类题目的核心区别：A 类判断的是客观事实信息，B 类判断的是作者的主观观点。答案写法不能混用。

---

## 二、判断题答案类型详解

### 1. 正确 (TRUE/YES) 的两种形式

#### a 类：字面意思匹配（同义替换）
- **题目**：City life is one factor that encourages the development of intelligence.（C7T3P1）

@@Whereas prehistoric man had no exposure to %%urban lifestyles%% - the %%forcing house%% of intelligence. The evidence suggests that ants have lived in %%urban settings%% for close on a hundred million years, developing and maintaining underground cities of specialised chambers and tunnels.@@

- **解析**：
  - `City life` ↔ `urban lifestyles / urban settings`（同义替换）
  - `encourages the development of intelligence` ↔ `the forcing house of intelligence`（forcing house = 促进...发展的环境）
  - 题干是原文的同义改写，答案为 **TRUE** ✅

#### b 类：归纳总结
- **题目**：Aphids' journeys are affected by changes in the light that they perceive.（C11T3P2）

@@They allow for the fact that, for example, %%aphids%% will become %%sensitive to blue light%% (from the sky) when it's time to %%take off on their big journey%%, and %%sensitive to yellow light%% (reflected from tender young leaves) when it's appropriate to %%land%%.@@

- **解析**：
  - 原文详细描述了蚜虫在起飞时对蓝光敏感、降落时对黄光敏感
  - 题干将这些细节 **归纳总结** 为"光线变化影响蚜虫的旅程"
  - 题干是对原文细节的概括，答案为 **TRUE** ✅

#### ✅ 正确形式小结
1. **字面意思**：题干与原文通过同义词/近义词替换表达相同含义
2. **归纳总结**：题干是对原文多个细节的概括性表述

---

### 2. 错误 (FALSE/NO) 的两种形式

#### c 类：反义词类型（直接矛盾）
- **题目**：Natural forests still exist in parts of Europe.（C7T3P3）

@@The myth of the 'natural' forest has survived, yet there are effectively %%no remaining%% 'primary' forests in Europe.@@

- **解析**：
  - 题干说"天然森林仍然存在"
  - 原文说"实际上**没有**剩余的原始森林"
  - `still exist` 与 `no remaining` 构成 **直接反义矛盾**
  - 答案为 **FALSE** ❌

#### d 类：有反例/与事实相违背
- **题目**：Ants use the same channels of communication as humans do.（C7T3P1）

@@Ants store food, repel attackers and use %%chemical signals%% to contact one another in case of attack. Such chemical communication can be compared to the human use of %%visual and auditory channels%% to arouse and propagate moods and attitudes.@@

- **解析**：
  - 题干说蚂蚁使用"**与人类相同的**"沟通渠道
  - 原文明确指出：蚂蚁用 **化学信号 (chemical signals)**，人类用 **视觉和听觉渠道 (visual and auditory channels)**
  - 两者使用的渠道 **不同**，题干与事实相违背
  - `the same` 是关键考点词，原文给出了反例
  - 答案为 **FALSE** ❌

#### ❌ 错误形式小结
1. **反义词类型**：题干中某个关键词与原文形成直接反义（如 exist vs. no remaining）
2. **有反例/违背事实**：原文给出的具体事实与题干的表述相矛盾

---

### 3. 未提及 (NOT GIVEN) 的两种形式

#### e 类：部分提及型（话题相关但信息缺失）
- **题目**：The essay, 'In the company of ants', explores ant communication.（C7T3P1）

@@It's no surprise that Edward Wilson, in his essay, %%'In the company of ants'%%, advises readers who ask what to do with the ants in their kitchen to: 'Watch where you step. Be careful of little lives.'@@

- **解析**：
  - 原文确实提到了这篇文章 *'In the company of ants'*（定位词匹配）
  - 但原文只说这篇文章建议读者小心蚂蚁，**没有提及**该文章是否"探讨蚂蚁的沟通方式"
  - 题干中 `explores ant communication` 这个信息在原文中 **找不到依据**
  - 答案为 **NOT GIVEN** ❓

#### f 类：完全未提及型（原文完全没有相关信息）
- **题目**：The number of people buying dolls has grown over the centuries.（C12T5P2）

@@Similarly, people who %%collect dolls%% may go beyond simply enlarging their collection, and develop an interest in the way that dolls are made, or the %%materials%% that are used. These have %%changed over the centuries%% from the wood that was standard in 16th century Europe, through the wax and porcelain of later centuries, to the plastics of today's dolls.@@

- **解析**：
  - 原文谈到了几个世纪以来 **制作娃娃的材料** 发生了变化
  - 题干问的是 **购买娃娃的人数** 是否增长——这个信息原文 **完全没有提及**
  - 题干偷换了概念：材料变化 ≠ 购买人数增长
  - 答案为 **NOT GIVEN** ❓

#### ❓ 未提及形式小结
1. **部分提及型**：定位词能找到，但题干的核心判断点在原文中没有信息
2. **完全未提及型**：题干讨论的内容与原文讨论的角度完全不同，无法判断真假

## 三、判断题解题步骤（三步法）

### 第一步：阅读题干，勾画 **定位词 + 考点词**
- 定位词：帮助在原文中快速找到对应位置（专有名词、数字、特殊表达等）
- 考点词：题干和原文中最有可能产生矛盾的词（通常1-2个）

### 第二步：扫读定位，注意 **顺序原则**
- 判断题的题目顺序与原文出现顺序 **基本一致**
- 利用顺序原则缩小定位范围

### 第三步：精读理解，注意 **同义替换和矛盾点**
- 对比题干和原文，关注考点词是否被同义替换、矛盾替换或未提及

> 📝 总结：做题步骤分"三步"——**画词 → 定位 → 比对**

## 四、实战练习题解析（The Pixar Story）

:::exam-left
**The Pixar Story**

*The animation studio Pixar has experienced a number of challenges in its short history*

Between 2000 and 2005, under former Chief Executive Officer (CEO) Michael D. Eisner, Walt Disney Company set its shareholder meetings in a series of cold-weather cities during cold-weather months. Many suspected that this had been to discourage attendance by the company's increasingly restless shareholders. In 2006, however, there was no need for such measures. In October 2005, Chief Operating Officer Robert Iger had replaced Eisner as CEO. On taking charge, Iger had quickly carried out a bold stroke, negotiating a buyout of Pixar Animation Studios. Although the price was high, the acquisition proved to be overwhelmingly popular.

Pixar had started life twenty years earlier as Pixar Inc., a computer hardware company, one of hundreds dotting the landscape of the San Francisco Bay Area in California. Within the company, a tiny animation group made short promotional films that brought in practically zero revenue, the group came close more than once to being shut down. When the Pixar Image Computer proved unprofitable, the company turned to selling animation software and making animated commercials, but it kept losing money year after year.

In 1991, when Disney agreed to finance the production of Pixar's first feature film for cinema release, Disney had virtually dictated the terms of the contract. In late 1993, Disney considered ordering Pixar to shut down production of the John Lasseter directed film, Toy Story, on account of issues with the script. At the time, Hollywood commentators doubted whether audiences would even sit through a full-length film in the new medium of computer animation. It was unclear whether computer animation would prove to be a novelty that would quickly wear thin.

Before long, however, things had changed dramatically. A succession of beloved and commercially successful animated feature films, starting with the 1995 release of Toy Story and including A Bug's Life and Finding Nemo, made Pixar the world's dominant animation studio. Disney made a fortune distributing the films. When Pixar's majority owner and CEO, Steve Jobs, cut off contract talks with Disney in early 2004, as the end of their distribution contract was in sight, the business press focused on the question of how Disney would cope. By then, it was Disney that seemed to need Pixar — the distribution of Pixar's films made up around 45% of the operating income from Disney's film operations. Disney's internal market research showed that mothers of young children trusted the Pixar brand more than Disney's.
:::end-left

:::exam-right
**Questions 1-5**

Do the following statements agree with the information given in Reading Passage 1?

Write **TRUE** if the statement agrees with the information, **FALSE** if the statement contradicts the information, **NOT GIVEN** if there is no information on this.

1. Robert Iger had a low opinion of Michael D. Eisner.

2. Pixar Inc. had extremely limited financial success in its early years.

3. There were difficulties during the production of the film Toy Story.

4. In the early 1990s, Hollywood commentators were convinced animated films had a promising future.

5. Finding Nemo was more popular than Toy Story.
:::end-right

### 题目解析

#### Q1: Robert Iger had a low opinion of Michael D. Eisner.
- **定位词**：Robert Iger, Michael D. Eisner
- **考点词**：`a low opinion of`（对...评价低）

@@In October 2005, Chief Operating Officer %%Robert Iger%% had replaced %%Eisner%% as CEO. On taking charge, Iger had quickly carried out a bold stroke, negotiating a buyout of Pixar Animation Studios.@@

- **分析**：原文只提到 Iger 接替 Eisner 成为CEO后收购了Pixar，没有任何关于 Iger 对 Eisner 看法/评价的信息
- **答案**：**NOT GIVEN** ❓

#### Q2: Pixar Inc. had extremely limited financial success in its early years.
- **定位词**：Pixar Inc., early years
- **考点词**：`extremely limited financial success`（极其有限的经济成功）

@@Pixar had started life twenty years earlier as Pixar Inc., a computer hardware company... a tiny animation group made short promotional films that brought in practically %%zero revenue%%, the group came close more than once to being shut down. When the Pixar Image Computer proved unprofitable, the company turned to selling animation software and making animated commercials, but it %%kept losing money%% year after year.@@

- **分析**：原文说几乎零收入、持续亏损 → 与"经济成功极其有限"含义一致（甚至更惨）
- **答案**：**TRUE** ✅

#### Q3: There were difficulties during the production of the film Toy Story.
- **定位词**：Toy Story
- **考点词**：`difficulties`（困难）

@@In late 1993, Disney considered ordering Pixar to %%shut down production%% of the John Lasseter directed film, Toy Story, on account of %%issues with the script%%.@@

- **分析**：剧本有问题 + 差点被叫停 = 制作过程中有困难，`issues` ↔ `difficulties` 同义替换
- **答案**：**TRUE** ✅

#### Q4: In the early 1990s, Hollywood commentators were convinced animated films had a promising future.
- **定位词**：early 1990s, Hollywood commentators
- **考点词**：`were convinced`（确信）、`a promising future`（前景光明）

@@Hollywood commentators %%doubted%% whether audiences would even sit through a full-length film in the new medium of computer animation. It was %%unclear%% whether computer animation would prove to be a %%novelty that would quickly wear thin%%.@@

- **分析**：
  - `convinced`（确信）↔ `doubted`（怀疑）→ **直接反义**
  - `promising future`（前景光明）↔ `novelty that would quickly wear thin`（很快失去新鲜感）→ **矛盾**
- **答案**：**FALSE** ❌

#### Q5: Finding Nemo was more popular than Toy Story.
- **定位词**：Finding Nemo, Toy Story
- **考点词**：`more popular than`（比...更受欢迎）→ 比较关系

@@A succession of %%beloved and commercially successful%% animated feature films, starting with the 1995 release of %%Toy Story%% and including A Bug's Life and %%Finding Nemo%%, made Pixar the world's dominant animation studio.@@

- **分析**：原文并列提及了这两部电影，都是"深受喜爱且商业成功的"，但 **没有对两者进行比较**
- **答案**：**NOT GIVEN** ❓

## 五、题型小规律

1. **答案书写**：建议写 **完整单词**（TRUE/FALSE/NOT GIVEN），不要缩写
2. **答案分布**：一般是 **均匀分布**（T、F、NG 各出现至少一次）；顺序规律一般是 **题目顺序与原文顺序一致**
3. 练习过程中，建议判断题 **全部题目一起做**，进行卡位和 NG 判定
4. 判断题最多连续 **2** 题答案一致
5. 如果文章只有 3 个判断题，则大概率答案分布为 **T、F、NG 各一个**

## 六、认识考点词

### 定义
考点词是指题干和原文中 **最有可能有矛盾的词或词组**，一般每个题目中考点词有 **1-2** 个。

### 考点词种类大总结

| 类型 | 名称 | 说明 | 常见词性 |
|------|------|------|----------|
| **A 类** | 反义考点词 | 题干与原文可能出现反义词替换 | 形容词、副词、介词、动词 |
| **B 类** | Be 动词考点词 | 关注 is/are/was 等后面的表语信息 | — |
| **C 类** | 特殊类型 I | 数字、程度词、趋势词 | — |
| **D 类** | 特殊类型 II | 因果关系词 | — |
| **F 类** | 特殊类型 III | 比较关系（双方、内容、关系） | — |

### A 类：反义考点词
- 最常见的考点类型
- 题干和原文中的形容词、副词、介词、动词可能出现反义替换
- 例如：`same` ↔ 不同的具体内容；`exist` ↔ `no remaining`

### B 类：Be 动词考点词
- 关注系动词后面的表语成分
- 判断主语的属性、身份、状态是否与原文一致

### C 类：特殊类型 I（数字/程度/趋势）
- **数字**：关注具体数字是否与原文一致
- **程度词**：如 all, most, some, only, mainly, primarily 等
- **趋势词**：如 increase, decrease, grow, decline, change 等

### D 类：特殊类型 II（因果关系）
- 关注题干中的因果连接词：because, due to, as a result of, lead to 等
- 判断原文中的因果关系是否与题干一致
- 常见陷阱：因果颠倒、原因错配

### F 类：特殊类型 III（比较关系）
- 比较的三要素：**双方**（比较对象）、**内容**（比较维度）、**关系**（more/less/same/different）
- 例如 Q5：Finding Nemo vs Toy Story（双方）、popular（内容）、more...than（关系）

### 考点词搜寻原则
1. **优先关注**：形容词、副词、动词、介词等容易产生矛盾的词
2. **搜寻位置**：一般在句子 **主干** 中寻找，而非修饰成分

## 七、词汇积累

| 例句中的加粗词 | 词性 | 词义 | 匹配定义 |
|----------------|------|------|----------|

| **promotional** | 形容词 | 宣传推广的 | A. designed to advertise something in order to sell it |
