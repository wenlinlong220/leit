# An Important Language Development 全题解析

:::exam-left
**An Important Language Development**

**A** Cuneiform, the world's first known system of handwriting, originated some 6,000 years ago in Sumer in what is now southern Iraq. It was most often inscribed on palm-sized, rectangular clay tablets measuring several centimetres across, although occasionally larger tablets or cylinders were used. Clay was an excellent medium for writing. Other surfaces which have been employed—for example, parchment, papyrus and paper—are not long-lasting and are easily destroyed by fire and water. But clay has proved to be resistant to those particular kinds of damage.

**B** The word 'cuneiform' actually refers to the marks or signs inscribed in the clay. The original cuneiform signs consisted of a series of lines—triangular, vertical, diagonal, and horizontal. Sumerian writers would impress these lines into the wet clay with a stylus—a long, thin, pointed instrument which looked somewhat like a pen. Oddly, the signs were often almost too small to see with the naked eye. Cuneiform signs were used for the writing of at least a dozen languages. This is similar to how the Latin alphabet is used today for writing English, French, Spanish and German, for example.

**C** Before the development of cuneiform, tokens were used by the Sumerians to record certain information. For example, they might take small stones and use them as tokens or representations of something else, like a goat. A number of tokens, then, might mean a herd of goats. These tokens might then be placed in a cloth container and provided to a buyer as a receipt for a transaction, perhaps five tokens for five animals. It was not that different from what we do today when we buy some bread and the clerk gives us back a piece of paper with numbers on it to confirm the exchange.

**D** By the 4th century BCE, the Sumerians had adapted this system to a form of writing. They began putting tokens in a container resembling an envelope, one made of clay instead of cloth. They then stamped the outside to indicate the number and type of tokens inside. A person could then 'read' what was stamped on the container and know what was inside.

**E** Gradually, Sumerians developed symbols for words. When first developed, each symbol looked like the concrete thing it represented. For example, an image which resembled the drawing of a sheep meant just that. Then another level of abstraction was introduced when symbols were developed for intangible ideas such as 'female' or 'hot' or 'God'. Cuneiform, in other words, evolved from a way used primarily to track and store information into a way to represent the world symbolically. Over the centuries, the marks became ever more abstract, finally evolving into signs that looked nothing like what they referred to, just as the letters 'h-o-u-s-e' have no visual connection to the place we live in. At this last stage in the evolution of cuneiform, the signs took the form of triangles, which became common cuneiform signs.

**F** As the marks became more abstract, the system became more efficient because there were fewer marks for a 'reader' to learn. But cuneiform also became more complex because society itself was becoming more complex, so there were more ideas and concepts that needed to be expressed. However, most linguists and historians agree cuneiform developed primarily as a tool for accounting. Of the cuneiform tablets that have been discovered, excavated and translated, about 75 percent contain this type of practical information, rather than artistic or imaginative work.

**G** Cuneiform writing was used for thousands of years, but it eventually ceased to be used in everyday life. In fact, it died out and remained unintelligible for almost 2,000 years. In the late 19th century, a British army officer, Henry Rawlinson, discovered cuneiform inscriptions which had been carved into the surface of rocks in the Behistun Mountains in what is present-day Iran. Rawlinson made impressions of the marks on large pieces of paper as he balanced dangerously on the surrounding rocks.

**H** Rawlinson took his copies home to Britain and studied them for years to determine what each line stood for, and what each group of symbols meant. He found that, in the writing on those particular rocks, every word was repeated three times in three languages: Old Persian, Elamite and Babylonian. Since the meanings in these languages were already known to linguists, he could thus translate the cuneiform. Eventually, he fully decoded the cuneiform marks and discovered that they described the life of Darius, a king of the Persian Empire in the 5th century BCE.

**Q1: Cuneiform tablets were produced in different shapes and sizes.**
- 定位词：`shapes`、`sizes`
- 原文（段A）：It was most often inscribed on **palm-sized, rectangular clay tablets**...although occasionally **larger tablets or cylinders** were used.
- 分析：

| 题目词 | 原文词 | 替换类型 |
|--------|--------|---------|
| different sizes | `palm-sized` vs `larger` | 对比两种大小 |
| different shapes | `rectangular` vs `cylinders` | 长方形 vs 圆柱形 |

- ⚠️ **关键词：`although`（尽管）**，引出"偶尔也用更大的泥板或圆柱形"，两者合在一起才能证明"不同形状和尺寸"
- ✅ 答案：**TRUE**

---

**Q2: When Sumerian writers marked the clay tablets, the tablets were dry.**
- 定位词：`Sumerian writers`、`clay tablets`、`marked`（找描述刻字动作和黏土状态的句子）
- 原文（段B）：Sumerian writers would impress these lines into the **wet clay** with a stylus
- 分析：`the tablets were dry`（干的）↔ `wet clay`（湿的黏土）→ **直接矛盾**
- 🧠 **做题技巧：遇到描述"状态/条件"的题，定位词用最具体的名词（`clay`），直接找原文描述黏土状态的词**
- ❌ 答案：**FALSE**

---

**Q3: Cuneiform was often difficult to read because of its size.**
- 定位词：`difficult to read`、`size`
- 原文（段B）：the signs were often almost **too small to see with the naked eye**
- 分析：`difficult to read` ↔ `too small to see`（太小以至于肉眼难辨），`because of its size` ↔ `small`（因为太小）
- ✅ 答案：**TRUE**

---

**Q4: A number of languages adopted cuneiform.**
- 定位词：`languages`、`adopted`
- 原文（段B）：Cuneiform signs were used for the writing of at least **a dozen languages**
- 分析：`a number of` ↔ `at least a dozen`（至少十几种），`adopted` ↔ `used for`（同义替换）
- ✅ 答案：**TRUE**

---

**Q5: Cuneiform signs can be found in some modern alphabets.**
- 定位词：`modern alphabets`、`cuneiform signs`
- 原文（段B）：This is similar to how the **Latin alphabet** is used today...（只是做类比，并不是说楔形文字出现在现代字母表里）
- 分析：原文说楔形文字**类似于**拉丁字母的使用方式（多语言共用），但**没有说**楔形文字符号出现在现代字母表中
- ❓ 答案：**NOT GIVEN**

**Q7: the first tokens were kept in containers made of _______**

**Q8: tokens were used as a _______ to give when selling something**
- 原文（段C）：provided to a buyer as a **receipt** for a transaction
- ⚠️ **常见错误：填了 `paper`**
  - 同段有一句比喻："the clerk gives us back a **piece of paper**..."
  - 这是用**现代收据**来比喻古代 token，**比喻句里的词不是答案**
  - 题目问的是"那个时代"的 token 起什么作用 → `receipt`（收据）
  - **规律：比喻句中用"今天/现代"举例，答案一定在比喻句之前的那句话里**
- ✅ 答案：**receipt**

---

**Q9: tokens were put in a container that looked like a clay _______**
- 原文（段D）：a container resembling an **envelope**, one made of clay instead of cloth
- ✅ 答案：**envelope**

---

**Q10: at first, signs looked like what they indicated, e.g. _______**
- 原文（段E）：an image which resembled the drawing of **a sheep** meant just that
- ⚠️ **常见错误：填了 `image`**
  - 原文句子结构：`an image`（符号）+ `resembled`（像）+ `the drawing of a sheep`（羊的图画）
  - 题目中 `e.g.` 后要填的是**符号代表的具体事物**，不是符号本身
  - `image` 是符号本身，`sheep` 才是符号所指向的事物
  - **规律：`look like what they indicated`（看起来像它们代表的东西）→ 答案是"被代表的东西"，即 `sheep`**
- ✅ 答案：**sheep**

---

**Q11: eventually, cuneiform signs shaped like _______ were developed**
- 原文（段E）：the signs took the form of **triangles**, which became common cuneiform signs
- ✅ 答案：**triangles**

---

**Q12: according to experts, cuneiform was mainly used for _______**
- 原文（段F）：most linguists and historians agree cuneiform developed primarily as a tool for **accounting**
- 分析：`according to experts` ↔ `most linguists and historians agree`，`mainly used for` ↔ `primarily as a tool for`
- ✅ 答案：**accounting**

---

**Q13: Rawlinson copied the inscriptions onto _______**
- 原文（段G）：Rawlinson made impressions of the marks on large pieces of **paper**
- ⚠️ **常见错误：填了 `Britain`**
  - 错误原因：定位到了下一段的首句：`Rawlinson took his copies home to **Britain**`
  - 题目问的是"复制到什么上面"，动作是"make impressions on..."，对应段G的 `paper`
  - `Britain` 是他**复制之后带回去的地方**，是下一步动作，不是答案
  - **规律：题目问"onto what"→ 找"on/onto"后面的名词，不要读到下一步动作**
- ✅ 答案：**paper**

### 句一：楔形文字定义句

**原句：**

Cuneiform, the world's first known system of handwriting, originated some 6,000 years ago in Sumer in what is now southern Iraq.

**句子结构：**

- 主干：Cuneiform originated（楔形文字起源于）
- 同位语：, the world's first known system of handwriting,（世界上已知最早的书写系统）
- 时间状语：some 6,000 years ago（大约6000年前）
- 地点状语：in Sumer（在苏美尔）
- 地点补充：in what is now southern Iraq（在如今的伊拉克南部）

**考点：**

- `what is now + 地名` → what = the place that，意为"如今的……"，用于处理古今地名不一致的问题

---

### 句二：泥板尺寸描述句

**原句：**

It was most often inscribed on palm-sized, rectangular clay tablets measuring several centimetres across, although occasionally larger ==tablets== or ==cylinders== were used.

**句子结构：**

- 主干：It was inscribed on...tablets（它被刻在泥板上）
- 频度状语：most often（最经常）
- 定语修饰 tablets：palm-sized, rectangular, clay（手掌大小、长方形、黏土制）
- 后置定语：measuring several centimetres across（宽达几厘米）
- 让步状语从句：although occasionally larger tablets or cylinders were used（虽然偶尔也使用更大的泥板或圆柱体）

**考点：**

- `名词 + measuring + 尺寸 + across` → 现在分词短语作后置定语，相当于 which measure...
- `measure` 熟词僻义：不及物动词，意为"尺寸为"
- 有一个larger tablets or cylinders 这一句话同时代表了尺寸和形状

---

### 句三：拓印动作伴随句

**原句：**

Rawlinson made impressions of the marks on large pieces of paper as he balanced dangerously on the surrounding rocks.

**句子结构：**

- 主干：Rawlinson made impressions（罗林森制作拓片）
- 宾语补充：of the marks on large pieces of paper（把刻痕拓印到大张纸上）
- 伴随状语从句：as he balanced dangerously on the surrounding rocks（一边在周围岩石上危险地保持平衡）

**考点：**

- `as` 引导时间/伴随状语，表示两个动作同时发生
- `make impressions of...on...` 固定搭配，意为"把……拓印到……上"
- `balance` 动词用法，意为"保持平衡"

---

### 句四：研究目的并列从句句

**原句：**

Rawlinson took his copies home to Britain and studied them for years to determine what each line stood for, and what each group of symbols meant.

**句子结构：**

- 并列谓语：took...and studied...
- 谓语一：took his copies home to Britain（把拓本带回英国的家）
- 谓语二：studied them for years（研究它们数年）
- 目的状语：to determine A and B（为了确定A和B）
- 宾语从句 A：what each line stood for（每一笔划代表什么）
- 宾语从句 B：what each group of symbols meant（每一组符号意味着什么）

**考点：**

- `home to + 地点` → ==home 是副词==，意为"回家/回国"，to 引出具体地点
- `what` 引导的名词性从句作宾语，内部用陈述句语序
- `stand for` 短语动词，意为"代表"
